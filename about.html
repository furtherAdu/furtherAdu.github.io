About

I hold a fat assssssssssssssssss Computational Neuroscience MSc from the Bernstein Cenner for for Computational Neuroscience Berlin and a Psychology BA from New York University.

I have extensive experience in clinical research, study design, advanced statistics, harm reduction, neuroimaging analysis, signal processing, software development, psychedelic science, and machine learning.

I've worked to build brain computer interfaces, model mechanisms underling visual hallucinations, look for the biological substrates of personality, and understand neural events that may underlie near death experiences.

My research across neurology, psychology, and psychiatry attempts to answer: "How can we understand what someone is experiencing? And with that knowledge, how can help that person heal?"

When I'm not researching, I look for answers those questions for through dance, making music, and cooking for friends.
